# Product

## Register

product

## Users

General job seekers — people actively applying across industries and levels. They open this extension while already on a job posting page, mid-task and focused. The context is high-stakes and often stressful: a specific role they want, a deadline looming, uncertainty about whether their application will land. They need results, not friction.

## Product Purpose

A Chrome extension that extracts job description details from any posting page, combines them with the user's stored profile, and generates tailored resume and cover letter drafts as downloadable .docx files. The AI handles the tailoring; the user reviews, refines, and exports. Success means a confident, ready-to-send application in under five minutes.

## Brand Personality

Calm, capable, trustworthy. The tone is that of a skilled career coach who knows what they're doing and doesn't need to prove it. Warm and encouraging without being cheerful or pressuring. Professional without being corporate. No flash — just competence and care.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI SaaS: dark mode with indigo/purple gradient glows, glassmorphism, animated accents. Looks like every AI wrapper tool from 2023.
- Corporate job-board/recruiter aesthetics: LinkedIn, Indeed, Workday. Cold, form-heavy, HR-portal energy.
- Colorful upsell-heavy resume builders: Canva, Zety, Resume.io. Gamified progress, pastel palettes, constant upgrade prompts.
- Generic productivity apps: Notion clones, Todoist-style. Soft neutrals, endless rounded cards, pastel accents.

## Design Principles

1. Reduce cognitive load at every step. The user is already stressed about the job application, not the tool. Every extra decision, warning, or ambiguous choice adds to that burden.
2. Surface trust before action. Be explicit about what the AI is doing, what data it is using, and what the output will look like. Trust is earned through transparency, not decoration.
3. Guided flow, not feature grid. The task is sequential: extract, review, generate, refine, export. The layout should reflect that sequence, not present everything at once.
4. Let the document be the hero. The preview is what matters. UI chrome should recede; the rendered draft should be front and center.
5. Honest affordances. Export paths should be clear and confident, not hedged with warnings. If a feature has a known limitation, surface it once at the right moment, not repeatedly on every button.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA as the baseline. Job seekers are a broad, general population: accessible defaults matter across age, vision, and motor ability. Support reduced motion. Keyboard navigability is important for a form-heavy tool.
